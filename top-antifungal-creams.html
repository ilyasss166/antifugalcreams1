How to Recognize a Fungal Infection

Before reaching for any cream, it's important to confirm you're dealing with a fungal issue. Look for these common symptoms:

  • Red, scaly patches that may ooze or crust
  • Intense itching, especially in warm, moist areas
  • Circular rashes with raised edges (classic ringworm sign)
  • Discolored nails that are thick or brittle
  • Burning sensation between toes (athlete's foot)
  • White, cottage cheese-like discharge in skin folds

Note: If symptoms persist beyond 2 weeks of treatment or worsen, consult a healthcare professional as you may need prescription-strength medication.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I use antifungal cream?

Continue treatment for 1-2 weeks after symptoms disappear to prevent recurrence. Most products recommend 4 weeks for nail fungus. Always follow package instructions or your doctor's advice.

Can I use antifungal cream on my face?

While generally safe, facial skin is more sensitive. Opt for creams labeled for sensitive skin (like Lotrimin Ultra) and avoid the eye area. If the rash persists or worsens, see a dermatologist as facial rashes often require precise diagnosis.

Creams absorb better for moist areas (between toes) while ointments protect dry, scaly patches. Powders work best for prevention in sweaty areas. Many find using a cream during the day and ointment at night provides optimal results.

Pro Tip from Dermatologists

To maximize effectiveness, wash and dry the affected area thoroughly before application. Use a clean cotton swab to apply (don't use fingers) to prevent spreading the infection. Wash hands immediately after application.
